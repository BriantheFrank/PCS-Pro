PCS Move Checklist

A practical, offline-friendly checklist to guide each phase of your Permanent Change of Station (PCS) move. A PCS is a military move to a new duty station.

This checklist is organized to support military families first. The spouse and family checklist appears first, followed by tasks that must be completed by the service member.

Disclaimer: This checklist is for planning purposes only and does not replace official guidance from your unit, transportation office, or finance office.

Spouse & Family Checklist

These tasks focus on daily life, household planning, and family routines. They often run in parallel with the service member's official requirements.

What: compare neighborhoods, waitlists, schools, and commute times for both on-post and off-post homes. Why: housing choices set timelines for school, child care, and move dates. Owner: Spouse/Family. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: align pack-out, pickup, and delivery dates once the transportation office confirms them. Why: the family needs child care, time off, and travel plans that match mover windows. Owner: Shared. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: list furniture, major items, and box counts by room. Why: inventories simplify claims, help find missing items, and keep everyone aligned on what is shipped versus hand-carried. Owner: Spouse/Family. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: photograph, note serial numbers, and separate fragile items or valuables. Why: documentation protects the family if items are lost or damaged. Owner: Spouse/Family. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: sort, declutter, and group items by room before movers arrive. Why: organized packing reduces delays, breakage, and confusion on delivery day. Owner: Spouse/Family. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: gather clothing, medications, documents, chargers, and kid or pet necessities that stay with the family. Why: this keeps everyone functioning while waiting for household goods delivery. Owner: Spouse/Family. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: request records, confirm enrollment requirements, and plan start dates. Why: children need minimal gaps in learning and care while the family is relocating. Owner: Spouse/Family. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: schedule vet visits, obtain health certificates, and book pet-friendly travel or lodging. Why: pet rules can block travel dates if not handled early. Owner: Spouse/Family. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: identify hotels, lodging facilities, or temporary rentals that fit the family and pets. Why: housing or deliveries can be delayed, so backup plans reduce stress. Owner: Spouse/Family. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: forward mail, change addresses for banks and medical providers, and update deliveries. Why: missed bills or supplies add stress during arrival week. Owner: Spouse/Family. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: plan meals, sleeping arrangements, and basic supplies in case furniture is delayed. Why: families often arrive before deliveries, so a simple plan keeps everyone comfortable. Owner: Spouse/Family. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

Service Member Checklist

These tasks must be handled by the service member because they involve official orders, finance, or command requirements. Either spouse can check items here so both can see progress.

What: confirm the move is official and read the dates and locations listed. Why: the family cannot schedule movers, housing, or schools without approved orders. Owner: Service Member. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: complete the unit or installation briefings tied to the move. Why: briefings unlock scheduling and benefits that the family depends on. Owner: Service Member. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: verify the date the service member must arrive and who is approved to travel. Why: this drives leave requests, housing timelines, and school planning. Owner: Service Member. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: confirm family members, addresses, and clearances in personnel systems. Why: errors can delay pay, travel approvals, or benefits the family needs. Owner: Service Member. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: ask about travel advances and the Dislocation Allowance (DLA), a one-time moving payment. Why: families often need upfront cash for deposits, lodging, or travel. Owner: Service Member. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: decide between a government-arranged move or a Personally Procured Move (PPM), which means the family moves themselves for reimbursement. Why: this decision changes dates, budgets, and who packs the house. Owner: Service Member. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: book mover pickup and delivery dates with the office that coordinates military shipments. Why: the family cannot finalize travel or lodging without confirmed dates. Owner: Service Member. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: collect required signatures, return issued gear, and close out local requirements. Why: without clearance, the family cannot depart on time. Owner: Service Member. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: request leave, set travel days, and align with required report dates. Why: travel approvals set when the family can depart and arrive. Owner: Service Member. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

What: submit the travel reimbursement form to the finance office after reaching the new duty station. Why: this repays travel expenses and closes out any advance the family used. Owner: Service Member. This step will later expand into a detailed checklist.

PCS Pitfalls and Pro Tips

  • Confirm medical and dental coverage transfers for the new location.
  • Ask early about special family needs screenings if applicable.
  • Review wills, powers of attorney, and family care plans if needed.
  • Check pay statements for changes after allowances update.
  • Keep digital copies of orders, receipts, and mover paperwork.
  • Share a calendar for mover dates, inspections, and school deadlines.
  • Take photos of high-value items before pack-out day.
