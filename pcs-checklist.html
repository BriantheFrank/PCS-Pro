PCS Move Checklist

A practical, offline-friendly checklist to guide each phase of your Permanent Change of Station move.

Disclaimer: This checklist is not official DoD guidance. Policies, rates, and procedures change annually. Always verify details with your TMO, finance office, and chain of command.

Receiving PCS Orders

Verify your orders match the assignment notification and store copies in your PCS binder.

Schedule the MPD or S1/G1 briefing early to unlock move scheduling in DPS and finance systems.

Confirm report timelines, en route TDY, and dependent travel entitlements before locking in leave and travel plans.

Address passports, command sponsorship, unaccompanied tours, or EFMP needs before transportation is scheduled.

Ensure personal data, dependents, and clearance information are current to prevent delays.

Ask about advance pay, GTCC use, and any required counseling for DLA or TLE.

Start medical and educational screenings right away if family members are enrolled.

Pre-Move Planning

Include MALT/mileage (roughly $0.22 per mile, changes annually), per diem (M&IE varies by location), DLA by rank/dependents, and TLE eligibility.

Compare estimates in DTS, DFAS, or Move.mil tools, and flag gaps early with your transportation or finance office.

Keep receipts in a PCS binder or digital folder to avoid under-claiming later.

Store orders, amendments, receipts, weight tickets, and contact rosters in one place.

Compare on-post and off-post options, waitlists, and neighborhood commute times.

Store copies of vaccinations, school transcripts, and vet documentation for easy check-in.

Pro tip: Consider adding an interactive entitlement calculator or official links to make estimates easier for families.

Scheduling Movers

Understand what the government arranges versus what you will self-procure for each portion of the move.

Reimbursement can be up to 100% of government cost, but requires counseling, weight tickets, and tax withholding awareness.

Verify authorized weight limits by grade and dependent status, including storage in transit or NTS if needed.

Coordinate DPS surveys, pack-out, and pickup windows with your transportation office.

Create a high-value inventory with serial numbers and photos to protect valuable items.

Separate aerosols, propane, lithium batteries, and other restricted items before pack-out.

Packing Period

Keep uniforms, documents, medications, and chargers with you for the travel days.

Ensure professional books and equipment are inventoried and weighed separately to protect your weight allowance.

Keep inventory lists and weight tickets ready for voucher submission and claims.

Segregate issued gear to make turn-in or inspection easier at clearing.

Keep vaccination records and airline paperwork in your hand-carry kit.

Capture photos and serial numbers to support DPS loss or damage claims.

Optional: Download branch-specific packing checklists to help families stay aligned.

Executing the PCS Move and Travel

The standard timeline is 1 travel day per 350 miles; build in safe rest and overnight stops.

If you have TDY en route, ensure DTS reservations, orders, and leave forms match your travel plan.

Verify passports, SOFA stamps, immunizations, and customs clearance timelines for overseas moves.

Review quarantine requirements (HI, Guam) and airline embargo windows early.

Coordinate CYS transfers, school enrollment, and EFMP travel needs for dependents.

Submit mail forwarding, build an emergency kit, and keep snacks and documents accessible during travel.

Clearing the Installation

Start DA Form 137-2 (or branch equivalent) and gather the required unit stamps.

Schedule final pay audits, travel advances, and counseling for DLA, TLE, or debt recoupment.

Update TRICARE region information and request records for dependents.

Provide lease termination notice, schedule inspections, and collect final housing clearance paperwork.

Clear TA-50 or other gear, and keep turn-in receipts for your records.

Ensure TAP requirements are documented if you are nearing ETS or retirement timelines.

Confirm dependent IDs, enrollments, and benefits remain active for the new duty station.

Complete VIN inspections, lienholder letters, and vehicle cleanliness requirements for shipping.

Clear rental gear, sports equipment, or lodging loans to avoid fees.

Save receipts for lodging, fuel, tolls, pet transport, and storage to support claims.

Arrival and Delivery

Report to your gaining command and confirm in-processing schedules as soon as you arrive.

Coordinate delivery dates and be present to inspect items at arrival.

Submit claims quickly using your move portal with photos and inventory notes.

Filing Your Travel Voucher

Submit your travel voucher soon after arrival to avoid pay delays and missed entitlements.

Attach orders, amendments, and travel documents in the system your finance office requires.

Include lodging, TLE, airfare, mileage logs, and weight tickets to support reimbursements.

Verify deductions for travel advances or GTCC charges before final payout.

Include pet travel claims, multiple POV mileage, laundry during delays, and follow up with finance as needed.

PCS Pitfalls and Pro Tips

  • Verify TRICARE region changes and re-enroll if required.
  • Start EFMP screening early to avoid assignment holds.
  • Prepare school impact aid forms and enrollment packets.
  • Schedule JAG updates for wills, POAs, and family care plans.
  • Review LES and TSP allocations after major pay changes.
  • Keep PPM weight tickets and fuel logs for audit readiness.
  • Store copies of orders and amendments in cloud storage.
