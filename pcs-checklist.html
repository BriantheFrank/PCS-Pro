PCS Move Checklist

A practical, offline-friendly checklist to guide each phase of your Permanent Change of Station (PCS) move.

Disclaimer: This checklist is for planning purposes only and does not replace official guidance from your unit, transportation office, or finance office.

Service Member Checklist

These tasks must be handled by the service member because they involve official orders, finance, or command requirements. Either spouse can check items here so both can see progress.

This is the official start of the move. The briefing unlocks scheduling and benefits, so it should happen as soon as orders are issued.

Confirms when the service member must arrive and who is approved to travel. Do this early so the family can plan leave, housing, and school timelines.

Keeping dependents, addresses, and clearances current prevents pay or benefits delays during the move.

Finance can provide upfront funds to cover deposits and travel costs. Ask early so the family has cash flow before departure.

This office books movers and pickup dates for government-arranged moves. Schedule as soon as orders are in hand.

Decide whether the government hires movers or the family moves themselves for reimbursement. The choice affects dates, budgeting, and paperwork.

The service member must collect required signatures and turn in issued gear before departure. Plan several days for this step.

Submitting the voucher (often on DD Form 1351-2) repays travel expenses and closes out advances. File within the timeline your finance office provides.

Spouse & Family Checklist

These tasks focus on daily life, household planning, and family routines. They often run in parallel with the service member's official requirements.

Start early to compare on-post and off-post neighborhoods, school zones, and commute times. This shapes the rest of the move plan.

Once the Transportation Office confirms dates, align child care, time off, and travel plans so someone is present for pickup and delivery.

Build this list before pack-out to simplify claims and help everyone remember what is shipped versus hand-carried.

During packing, label rooms clearly and set aside items the family needs immediately when arriving (linens, basics, paperwork).

Prepare this right before travel with medications, documents, snacks, and chargers so the family can function while waiting for delivery.

Request records and enrollment packets early, then confirm new school start dates before travel.

Some locations require health certificates or airline bookings. Start this weeks ahead so pets can travel on time.

Two to three weeks before departure, forward mail and update deliveries so bills and essentials arrive at the right place.

If housing or deliveries are delayed, knowing your lodging options reduces stress and helps the family settle faster.

PCS Pitfalls and Pro Tips

  • Confirm medical and dental coverage transfers for the new location.
  • Ask early about special family needs screenings if applicable.
  • Review wills, powers of attorney, and family care plans if needed.
  • Check pay statements for changes after allowances update.
  • Keep digital copies of orders, receipts, and mover paperwork.
  • Share a calendar for mover dates, inspections, and school deadlines.
  • Take photos of high-value items before pack-out day.
