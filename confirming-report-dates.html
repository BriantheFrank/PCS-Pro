PCS Checklist Step

Confirming Report Dates and Authorized Travelers

Make sure your timeline and travel approvals match your orders.

← Back to PCS checklist

Why this step matters

Your report date controls everything: move dates, school transitions, child care plans, and travel bookings. Confirming who can travel with you prevents costly last-minute changes.

Detailed sub-checklist

Why: some orders allow a window for early reporting. Ask your unit if adjusting the date is possible before booking travel.

Why: only listed dependents are covered for travel allowances. Verify spelling and status within the first week of receiving orders.

Why: some families travel separately because of school or work. Get approval before buying tickets or scheduling movers.

Why: leave windows affect travel time and lodging reimbursements. Align leave requests 30-60 days out if possible.

Why: this helps children and workplaces plan for transitions and prevents sudden schedule changes.

Common pitfalls & pro tips

  • Booking flights before confirming who is authorized to travel. Get the approvals first.
  • Forgetting to coordinate leave with school calendars. Summer moves are easier but not always possible.
  • Keep a quick timeline in your phone with key dates and contacts.
  • If traveling separately, document the plan so both households know who carries which documents and essentials.
