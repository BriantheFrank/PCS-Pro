PCS Checklist Step

Attending PCS Briefings and Workshops

These sessions fill in the gaps that orders do not cover.

← Back to PCS checklist

Why this step matters

Briefings are where you learn the real-world details: mover timelines, finance paperwork, housing waitlists, and local tips. They save time by answering questions before you make costly decisions.

Detailed sub-checklist

Why: some installations require a PCS briefing before scheduling movers. Ask your unit or installation website within the first week after getting orders.

Why: sessions fill quickly, especially in summer. Booking 60-90 days out keeps your timeline on track.

Why: presenters can answer specific questions about entitlements and dependents when you have the paperwork ready.

Why: housing, transportation, and finance offices all have different timelines. Keeping one list prevents missed steps.

Why: briefings often include community resources that reduce stress for families. Ask for local points of contact.

Why: everyone should understand the timeline and the next decisions. A quick recap keeps spouses and partners aligned.

Common pitfalls & pro tips

  • Skipping briefings because the service member attended one. Spouses often need the same information for housing and schools.
  • Not writing down the transportation office or housing contact. Save phone numbers during the briefing.
  • Bring headphones if joining virtually from home with kids.
  • If you cannot attend live, ask for a recording or slides so you can still capture the details.
