Receiving and Reviewing PCS Orders

Know what your orders say and what decisions they kick off.

← Back to PCS checklist

Why this step matters

Getting PCS orders is the moment everything becomes real. Reading them carefully gives your family the timeline, entitlements, and travel rules you need so you can plan without surprises.

Detailed sub-checklist

Why: this tells you exactly when and where your service member must report. Timing drives school end dates, move logistics, and travel bookings. Aim to review within 24-48 hours of receiving orders.

Why: names on orders affect travel allowances and housing eligibility. If anyone is missing, request a correction right away.

Why: the move type shapes how you schedule household goods and what costs you can claim. Clarify early if you want a Personally Procured Move (PPM).

Why: orders sometimes include special instructions for school start dates, housing assignments, or medical requirements. Note them so nothing is missed.

Why: you will use orders for housing, schools, travel, and finance. Keep one copy with your travel documents and one in cloud storage.

Why: orders trigger appointments for transportation, medical, finance, and briefings. Start a quick task list within the first week.

Common pitfalls & pro tips

  • Waiting too long to review: orders often contain time-sensitive steps like scheduling movers.
  • Assuming dependents are automatically covered. Always verify names and spelling before you book travel.
  • Keep a copy in your phone or email for quick access at offices or schools.
  • If the report date conflicts with school or childcare, ask the unit about options before making big commitments.
