Applying for Advance Pay and Financial Entitlements

Know what money is available and when it arrives.

← Back to PCS checklist

Why this step matters

PCS moves come with upfront costs like deposits, travel, and temporary lodging. Understanding advance pay and entitlements helps you plan a realistic budget and avoid unnecessary debt.

Detailed sub-checklist

Why: entitlements vary by location and family size. Start this conversation 60-90 days out so you can plan deposits and travel.

Why: advance pay is a loan against future pay. Use it for essential costs like temporary lodging or fuel, not routine bills.

Why: finance offices often need orders, leave dates, and command approval. Ask for a checklist so nothing delays processing.

Why: knowing your likely reimbursement range helps you budget for hotel stays, meals, and mileage.

Why: receipts are needed for travel vouchers later. A single folder prevents last-minute scrambles after arrival.

Common pitfalls & pro tips

  • Assuming advance pay is automatic. It must be requested and approved.
  • Forgetting that advance pay is repaid over time. Build repayment into your monthly budget.
  • If you are dual-military or dual-career, clarify which entitlements apply to each spouse.
  • Keep copies of submitted forms in case finance needs follow-up.
